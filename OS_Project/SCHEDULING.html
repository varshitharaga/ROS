Rate Monotonic Scheduling:
Rate Monotonic Scheduling (RMS) is a real-time scheduling algorithm that assigns higher priority to tasks
with shorter periods. This ensures that tasks with more frequent execution requirements are given precedence,
helping to meet timing constraints in real-time systems.Click Here
Deadline Monotonic Scheduling:
Deadline Monotonic Scheduling (DMS) assigns tasks priorities based on their deadlines, with earlier
deadlines receiving higher priority. This ensures tasks are executed timely, minimizing the risk of missing
deadlines in real-time systems.Click Here
Earliest Deadline First:
Earliest Deadline First (EDF) scheduling assigns priority to tasks based on their deadlines, with tasks having
the earliest deadlines given the highest priority. This approach aims to ensure that tasks are completed before
